    Section 3.2 Properties of Determinants

    In this section, we will see how to compute the determinant of a matrix inverse, which also tells us about the invertibility of a matrix.
    🔗

    Objectives

    After this section, students will be able to:
    🔗
    • Apply the multiplicative property of determinants
      🔗
      🔗
    • determine whether a matrix is invertible by computing its determinant
      🔗
      🔗
    🔗
    The most important theorem about determinants is this one:
    🔗

    Theorem 3.2.1. Determinants and Products.

    Suppose \(A\) and \(B\) are \(n \times n\) matrices. Then
    \begin{equation*} \det (AB) = \det A \det B \end{equation*}
    That is, the determinant of the product is the product of the determinants.
    🔗
    🔗
    And one consequence of this is the following Lemma:
    🔗

    Lemma 3.2.2.

    Let \(A\) be an \(n \times n\) matrix, then \(\det A = 0\) if and only if \(A\) has no inverse (hence \(A\) is not invertible).
    🔗
    🔗

    Example 3.2.3.

    We know that
    \begin{equation*} \begin{bmatrix} 5 \amp -2 \\ -9 \amp 5 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 5 \amp 0 \\ -9 \amp 1 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 1 \amp \frac{-2}{5} \\ 0 \amp \frac{7}{5} \end{bmatrix} \end{equation*}
    We can calculate the determinant of all three matrices and quickly verify that
    \begin{equation*} \begin{vmatrix} 5 \amp -2 \\ -9 \amp 5 \end{vmatrix} = \begin{vmatrix} 5 \amp 0 \\ -9 \amp 1 \end{vmatrix} \begin{vmatrix} 1 \amp \frac{-2}{5} \\ 0 \amp \frac{7}{5} \end{vmatrix} \end{equation*}
    🔗
    🔗
    If we know that \(A\) is invertible, then we can use the above theorem to compute the determinant of the inverse of \(A\text{.}\) Since \(AA^{-1} = I\text{,}\) we have
    \begin{align*} \det (AA^{-1}) \amp= \det I \\ \implies \qquad \det A \det A^{-1} \amp= 1 \\ \implies \qquad \det A^{-1} \amp= \frac{1}{\det A} \end{align*}
    Hence, \(\det\lp A^{-1} \rp = \dfrac{1}{\det A}\)
    🔗
    The last theorem in this section is about the determinant of a transpose. Long story short, the determinant of a matrix is the same as the determinant of its transpose. That is,
    \begin{equation*} \det \lp A^T \rp = \det A \end{equation*}
    🔗
